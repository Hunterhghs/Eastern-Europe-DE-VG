Three Eras, One Map

Era 1: Nation-Building

1918–1945
  • Found institutions from imperial rubble
  • Land reform: radical vs. gradual
  • Rail gauge choice — 100-year path dependency
  • Ethnic accommodation or centralization

Era 2: Planned Development

1945–1990
  • Industrialize under external constraint
  • 30% policy autonomy — shape how, not whether
  • Suppressed tensions accumulate invisibly
  • Environmental damage builds silently

Era 3: Convergence or Drift

1990–2025
  • System collapse — industrial legacy now a liability
  • Full policy autonomy, full accountability
  • EU accession track opens
  • Every Era 1+2 decision comes due

Core Systems

The D-Coefficient

Master Variable · 0–100

How fast capability diffuses from cities to villages. Five sub-indices: infrastructure, institutions, human capital, information, geography. Era-adjusted weights.

Convergence Maps

6 Attractor Basins

Phase space with gravitational pulls. Failed State Repeller → Extractive Growth Trap → Middle-Income Trap → Liberal/Coordinated/Developmental State attractors.

Institutional DNA

Depth × Quality

Institutions have depth (years operating) and quality (effectiveness). Deep + high quality = resilient. Deep + low quality = trap. Shallow = fragile but reformable.
